Our research aims to understand blood flow behaviour in healthy aging, exercise, and across disease states.

We focus on: 1) micro- and macro-vascular network topology, morphology, and function in peripheral and cerebral vascular beds, 2) investigating relationships between cardiovascular and cerebrovascular function in healthy and at-risk populations, and relationships between vascular function and cognitive outcomes, 3) AI-based (i.e., machine and deep learning) approaches to vascular imaging. We use multi-modal approaches (e.g., duplex and transcranial Doppler ultrasound, MRI, functional near infra-red spectroscopy with diffusion correlation spectroscopy, pupillometry) to better visualize and assess vascular responses to various stimuli.

Current/recent projects in the lab (last updated Jan. 29, 2021):

  • Characterizing cerebrovascular and cardiovascular disease risk in older adults and osteoarthritis patients
  • Characterizing cerebrovascular dysfunction in cardiac patients
  • Micro- and macrovascular vascular network feature analyses
  • Automation of cerebrovascular imaging analyses
  • Cerebrovascular segmentation and classification from MRI images
  • Large-scale physiological testing online
